Anxiety Management Guide

Coping Techniques

5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Exercise

Use your senses to ground yourself in the present moment:

5 things you can see
4 things you can touch
3 things you can hear
2 things you can smell
1 thing you can taste

STOP Technique

  • S - Stop what you're doing
  • T - Take a step back
  • O - Observe your thoughts and feelings
  • P - Proceed mindfully

Anxiety Triggers

Common Triggers

Identifying your triggers is the first step to managing them:

Work Deadlines Social Situations Health Concerns Financial Stress Conflict Change Perfectionism Time Pressure

Trigger Journal

Track your anxiety triggers with these prompts:

  • What was happening when you felt anxious?
  • What thoughts were going through your mind?
  • What physical sensations did you experience?
  • How intense was the anxiety (1-10)?
  • What helped you cope with it?

Relaxation Methods

Box Breathing

Follow the circle to practice box breathing:

Inhale
4 seconds

Progressive Muscle Relaxation

Systematically tense and relax each muscle group:

  1. Tense the muscle group (5 seconds)
  2. Release the tension (10 seconds)
  3. Notice the difference in sensation

Start with your toes and work up to your face.
