Sleep Hygiene Guide

Sleep Schedules

Why is a consistent sleep schedule important?

A regular sleep schedule helps regulate your body's internal clock (circadian rhythm), making it easier to fall asleep and wake up naturally.

Set Fixed Sleep and Wake Times

Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on weekends.

Plan for 7-9 Hours

Ensure your schedule allows for 7-9 hours of sleep each night.

Adjust Gradually

Make small 15-minute adjustments to your sleep schedule when needed.

Bedtime Routines

Creating a Relaxing Routine

A consistent bedtime routine signals to your body that it's time to wind down and prepare for sleep.

Digital Sunset

Stop using electronic devices 1-2 hours before bed to reduce blue light exposure.

Relaxation Activities

Read a book, practice gentle stretching, or try meditation before bed.

Avoid Heavy Meals

Finish eating at least 2-3 hours before bedtime.

Sleep Environment

Optimizing Your Sleep Space

Your bedroom environment plays a crucial role in the quality of your sleep.

Temperature Control

Keep your bedroom cool (around 65-68°F or 18-20°C).

Dark and Quiet

Use blackout curtains and consider white noise if needed.

Comfortable Bedding

Invest in a supportive mattress and comfortable pillows.
